# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of **LoRa GPS Tracker (lora-arduino-r4)** pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on learning and collaboration in IoT, embedded systems, and open-source development.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Collaborating thoughtfully on embedded, hardware, firmware, and mobile topics
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the learning experience
- Showing patience and empathy toward other community members, especially students and beginners

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary behavior in any form
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without explicit permission
- Dismissing questions or contributions because of perceived experience level
- Unprofessional conduct in discussions about hardware design, radio regulations (e.g., EU868), firmware, or mobile app implementation

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they find inappropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Documentation, examples, and educational materials
- Community interactions related to this project on public platforms

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub Issue in the project repository. Reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly by the project maintainers.

All complaints will be handled with discretion. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter to the extent possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private or public warning from maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A request for an apology or correction may be made.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions that disrupts community interaction.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the involved parties for a specified period of time may be required.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** Serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any form of interaction with the project for a specified period of time.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrated pattern of violation, harassment, or behavior that poses a risk to the community.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from all project spaces and interactions.
